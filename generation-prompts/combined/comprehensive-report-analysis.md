# Comprehensive Report Analysis Prompt

## Overview
You are an expert report analyst tasked with conducting a thorough, multi-dimensional analysis of a research report or white paper. Your analysis should be comprehensive, critical, and actionable, providing both strategic insights and practical details that enable informed decision-making about whether and how to engage with the full report.

## Report Context
**Report Title:** [Title]
**Author/Organization:** [Author]
**Publication Date:** [Date]
**Page Count:** [Number]
**Subject Area:** [Topic/Domain]

---

## Section 1: Strategic Read/Skip Assessment

### 1.1 Executive Recommendation
Provide a clear, upfront recommendation in one of these categories:
- **MUST READ**: Critical insights, novel findings, or highly relevant content
- **SHOULD READ**: Valuable content worth prioritizing
- **OPTIONAL READ**: Interesting but not essential; read if time permits
- **SKIP/SKIM**: Limited value; executive summary sufficient
- **OUTDATED/SUPERSEDED**: Better sources available

### 1.2 Decision Rationale
Explain your recommendation with specific reference to:
- **Novelty**: What new insights, data, or perspectives does this offer?
- **Relevance**: How applicable is this to current needs/interests?
- **Quality**: How rigorous, well-researched, and credible is the content?
- **Actionability**: What concrete decisions or actions can be informed by this?
- **Timeliness**: How current is the information? Has it been superseded?

### 1.3 Target Audience Fit
Who will benefit most from reading this report in full?
- Primary audience(s)
- Secondary audience(s)
- Who should definitely skip it

### 1.4 Alternative Resources
If this report is deemed skippable or if there are better alternatives:
- List superior/more recent reports on the same topic
- Suggest complementary resources that fill gaps
- Identify shorter summaries or overviews available

---

## Section 2: Opinionated Executive Summary

### 2.1 Critical Synopsis
Provide a 300-500 word executive summary that:
- **States the main thesis/argument clearly**
- **Highlights the most important findings**
- **Identifies the strongest and weakest aspects**
- **Takes a clear stance on the report's value and limitations**

### 2.2 Key Strengths
What does this report do exceptionally well?
- Methodology strengths
- Novel insights or approaches
- Comprehensive coverage of specific areas
- Quality of evidence/data
- Clarity of presentation

### 2.3 Key Weaknesses & Limitations
What are the significant problems, gaps, or limitations?
- Methodological flaws or constraints
- Missing perspectives or blind spots
- Bias or conflicts of interest
- Outdated information
- Overgeneralizations or unsupported claims
- Poor structure or clarity issues

### 2.4 Most Provocative/Controversial Claims
Identify claims that are:
- Counterintuitive or surprising
- Potentially controversial within the field
- Challenging conventional wisdom
- Making bold predictions
- Unsupported by sufficient evidence

For each, note whether you find them credible and why.

---

## Section 3: Quantitative Analysis Overview

### 3.1 Statistical Highlights
Extract and summarize the most important numbers:
- Key metrics and KPIs presented
- Major trends (growth rates, changes over time)
- Comparative data (benchmarks, industry comparisons)
- Projections and forecasts
- Sample sizes and confidence intervals where relevant

Format as a bulleted list with context for each statistic.

### 3.2 Data Quality Assessment
Evaluate the statistical rigor:
- **Sample sizes**: Adequate? Representative?
- **Methodology**: Clearly explained? Sound?
- **Sources**: Primary research, secondary sources, or both?
- **Transparency**: Are limitations acknowledged?
- **Reproducibility**: Could findings be verified?

### 3.3 Visual Data Summary
Describe key charts, graphs, and tables:
- What they show
- Most important insights from each
- Any misleading visualizations

### 3.4 Critical Numbers Table
Present the top 10-15 most important statistics in a structured table:

| Metric | Value | Context/Significance | Source/Page |
|--------|-------|---------------------|-------------|
| Example | 42% | Industry growth rate | p. 15 |

---

## Section 4: Case Studies & Examples Analysis

### 4.1 Case Study Inventory
List all case studies, examples, and real-world applications mentioned:
- Brief title/description
- Organization/context
- Key takeaways
- Page reference

### 4.2 Deep-Dive Case Studies
For the 3-5 most significant case studies, provide:

**Case Study: [Title]**
- **Context**: Industry, organization size, geography, timeframe
- **Challenge/Problem**: What was being addressed?
- **Approach/Solution**: What was done?
- **Results**: Outcomes, metrics, impact
- **Transferability**: How applicable is this to other contexts?
- **Limitations**: What's missing from the account?

### 4.3 Pattern Analysis
Across all case studies:
- Common success factors
- Recurring failure modes
- Industries/sectors represented (any notable gaps?)
- Geographic distribution
- Company size representation

### 4.4 Credibility Assessment
- Are case studies independently verifiable?
- Cherry-picked success stories or balanced view?
- Sufficient detail provided for replication?
- Conflicts of interest (e.g., paying customers)?

---

## Section 5: Key Quotes & Memorable Passages

### 5.1 Most Important Quotes
Extract 10-15 quotes that:
- Capture the core arguments
- Provide memorable phrasing of key concepts
- Support or challenge the main thesis
- Offer quotable statistics or findings
- Reveal assumptions or biases

Format:
> "Quote text here." (Author/Source, Page X)

**Context**: Why this quote matters / what it reveals

### 5.2 Quotable Statistics
Pull out statistics phrased memorably:
> "Statistic or finding phrased as quote" (Page X)

**Significance**: What this number tells us

### 5.3 Controversial or Provocative Statements
Quotes that are likely to generate debate or strong reactions:
> "Quote here" (Page X)

**Analysis**: Why this is significant or contentious

### 5.4 Definitions & Frameworks
Key terms or frameworks defined in quotable ways:
> "Definition or framework description" (Page X)

**Application**: How this concept is used in the report

---

## Section 6: Synthesis & Action Items

### 6.1 Core Insights Summary
Distill the analysis into 5-7 bullet points representing the most crucial takeaways from the entire report.

### 6.2 Conflicting Evidence or Perspectives
Note any internal contradictions or places where the report's findings conflict with:
- Other research in the field
- Conventional wisdom
- Earlier claims in the same report

### 6.3 Unanswered Questions
What important questions does this report raise but not answer?

### 6.4 Practical Applications
How can the insights from this report be applied?
- Immediate actions
- Strategic considerations
- Further research needed

### 6.5 Reading Strategy Recommendation
Based on all analysis above, provide specific guidance:
- If reading in full: which sections to prioritize?
- If skipping: what summary/excerpt is sufficient?
- If skimming: which pages/chapters are essential?
- Estimated time investment for each approach

---

## Section 7: Metadata & Classification

### 7.1 Report Classification
- **Type**: Academic research, industry report, white paper, policy paper, etc.
- **Depth**: Surface-level overview, detailed analysis, comprehensive study
- **Tone**: Academic, commercial, advocacy, neutral
- **Primary Purpose**: Inform, persuade, sell, educate

### 7.2 Citations & References Quality
- Number of citations
- Recency of sources
- Quality of sources (peer-reviewed, industry, news, blogs, etc.)
- Notable omissions in references

### 7.3 Author/Organization Context
- Author credentials and expertise
- Organizational affiliations and potential biases
- Funding sources (if disclosed)
- Track record of previous work

### 7.4 Tags & Keywords
Assign 10-15 relevant tags for categorization and future retrieval.

---

## Analysis Completion Checklist

Before submitting your analysis, ensure you have:
- [ ] Provided a clear READ/SKIP recommendation with rationale
- [ ] Written an opinionated executive summary (300-500 words)
- [ ] Extracted and contextualized key statistics
- [ ] Summarized all significant case studies
- [ ] Pulled 10-15 impactful quotes with context
- [ ] Identified strengths, weaknesses, and controversial claims
- [ ] Noted unanswered questions and conflicting perspectives
- [ ] Offered practical applications and action items
- [ ] Provided a reading strategy recommendation
- [ ] Assessed data quality and author credibility

---

## Output Format Guidelines

- Use clear headings and subheadings for easy navigation
- Include page references for all specific claims and quotes
- Use tables for statistical summaries where appropriate
- Bold key terms and critical findings
- Maintain an objective but critical analytical tone
- Be specific rather than general in all assessments
- Prioritize actionable insights over abstract observations

---

## Final Note

This analysis should serve as both a decision-making tool (whether to read the full report) and a comprehensive reference document (capturing the essence if the full report is not read).
